MY INTERESTS

The Core of My Passion

Gaming & Systems Thinking: From Counter-Strike to Redstone

Gaming has always been a central part of my life, shaping not only how I spend my free time but also how I think and create. I enjoy a wide range of games, from strategic real-time battles to fast-paced FPS titles like Counter-Strike. These games challenge my decision-making, coordination, teamwork, and adaptability — skills that I carry into my work and daily life.

But the game that influenced me the most is Minecraft. What really changed everything for me was discovering Minecraft Redstone. Learning how Redstone circuits worked — how signals move, how timing can be controlled, and how logic gates operate — was my first real exposure to systems thinking. Redstone was my entry point into building mechanisms, automating processes, and understanding how rules and logic can create something engaging. That experience is what led me toward game development. It showed me that behind every great game is a foundation of mechanics, systems, and ideas working together.

Interactive System Design

Because of this, I developed a deep love for designing interactive systems. I enjoy understanding why certain mechanics feel satisfying, how players respond to challenges, and how small design choices can completely change an experience. My studies at CIIT College of Arts and Technology continue to strengthen this perspective, giving me the tools to turn ideas into something real.

Technical Problem-Solving

I also have a strong interest in automation, scripting, and problem-solving. The same curiosity that Redstone sparked shows up in the way I create tools, build scripts, and design systems that make tasks easier or more efficient. I enjoy the process of figuring out how something works, breaking it down, and turning it into a working solution.

Math and logical thinking also play a significant role in my interests. I enjoy learning topics like trigonometry, algebra, and linear algebra because they give me a deeper understanding of how systems behave. These subjects strengthen my approach to both game mechanics and technical problem-solving.

Design & UI/UX

On the creative side, I’m drawn to UI and UX design, especially when it comes to crafting clean, organized interfaces. Projects like my productivity tool concept, Applesauce, allow me to combine logic, design, and practicality in a way that feels both expressive and useful.

Connection & Collaboration

Beyond games and development, I genuinely enjoy meeting people and interacting with them. I like hearing different perspectives, learning from conversations, and sharing ideas. Whether it’s through gaming, school projects, or creative collaborations, connecting with others is something I value. For me, the best experiences — whether in games or real life — come from the people you share them with.

Altogether, my interests form a blend of creativity, logic, curiosity, and connection. Gaming, Redstone, automation, design, math, and social interaction are all part of who I am and influence the kind of developer I’m becoming. Each experience, whether inside a game or in a classroom, contributes to my passion for building interactive digital worlds and creating meaningful experiences for others.
