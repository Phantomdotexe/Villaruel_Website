MY HOBBIES

I’ve always believed that hobbies are more than just ways to pass time — they are extensions of who we are, small pieces of identity that express our tastes, passions, and curiosities.

Archive Aesthetic & Identity

Fashion holds a special place in my world. I’m drawn to the aesthetic of early 2000s to early 2010s — especially the mood and shape of archive fashion and pieces inspired by designers like Rick Owens. I appreciate clothes not just as garments, but as statements, expressions of style, identity, and mood.

Scouring thrift shops, hunting for unique pieces, and imagining outfits that reflect my personal vibe — that’s where I find joy in fashion. Every shirt, jacket, or pair of shoes tells a story, and reworking or mixing those stories into something new appeals to me deeply.

Logic, Finds, and Building

Hands-On Tinkering

I enjoy hands-on tinkering. Whether I’m experimenting with scripts, automations, or small technical projects, there’s something satisfying about building — or fixing — things with my own hands (or my own code). Tinkering reminds me that I can create, improve, and play around with ideas until they work.

The Thrill of Thrifting

Thrifting is another hobby I genuinely love. There’s a certain thrill in exploring second-hand stores, browsing through racks, and discovering pieces that have had lives of their own. It’s a mix of treasure hunting, nostalgia, and sustainability that appeals to me. It goes hand in hand with my fashion interest: reimagining older pieces, giving them new life, and making them part of my daily style.

Time with Friends & Gaming

Lastly — and perhaps most importantly — I value spending time with friends. Life gets busy, deadlines and work pile up, but nothing replaces the energy of hanging out, laughing, sharing stories, and just being in good company. Whether it’s a casual meet-up, a gaming session, or a spontaneous thrift run, time with friends grounds me, refreshes me, and reminds me why balance matters.

Gaming also remains part of who I am. It started as a pastime — a way to unwind, explore, and connect with friends — and though life’s responsibilities and workload sometimes pull me away, the urge never fully goes away. Gaming has taught me about challenge, strategy, cooperation, and immersion.
